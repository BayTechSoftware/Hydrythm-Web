# Hydrythm Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** March 20, 2026

Hydrythm is designed for local-first device control. You can use core local features without creating an account.

## Information Hydrythm handles

Hydrythm may handle the following information when you use the app:

- Device names and labels that you enter
- Local device discovery and connection details on your WiFi network
- Device status and telemetry displayed in the app
- Notification preferences you choose inside the app
- Configuration files you export, import, or share at your request
- App version and build information used for release and support visibility

## What stays local

Hydrythm is local-first for current mobile functionality. That means:

- Local device control does not require mandatory sign-in
- Basic control and monitoring work on your local network
- Firmware updates are delivered from a public release feed and applied directly to the device on your local network

## Permissions

Hydrythm may request:

- Local network access so it can discover and communicate with Hydrythm devices
- Notification access so it can show device alerts on this phone
- Photo library access if iOS requires it for system file-handling paths used by the app

You can deny some permissions, but related features may not work correctly.

## Data sharing

Hydrythm does not intentionally sell personal data.

Hydrythm shares data only when needed to provide requested functionality, such as:

- Communicating with a Hydrythm device on your local network
- Downloading a published firmware package from the public Hydrythm release repository
- Exporting or sharing a configuration file when you request it
- Opening external links such as this policy

If future versions add account systems, remote access, analytics, or cloud telemetry, this policy will be updated accordingly.

## Retention

Device data and preferences handled by the app may remain on your device until you remove the app data or exported files. Public firmware release files remain in the public Hydrythm release repository until they are replaced or removed.

## Security

Hydrythm depends in part on the security of your local network, your device configuration, and the permissions you choose to grant. You are responsible for protecting the WiFi network and devices you use with Hydrythm.

## Contact

## Changes

This policy may be updated as the app or release flow changes. The current version is the one published in this repository.
