ReefIQ

Reef Aquarium Ecosystem Control App

What is ReefIQ?

ReefIQ is a mobile application developed by BayTech Software for reef aquarium hobbyists. The app provides a unified workspace to control ReefIQ wavemaker devices, monitor water chemistry parameters from Neptune APEX controllers, log manual test results, track parameter history over time, and receive AI-powered reef keeping guidance through text and voice conversations.

ReefIQ connects to devices on your local WiFi network for real-time control and monitoring. Optional cloud features (powered by Firebase) enable tank profile sync, parameter history storage, and the AI assistant. The AI assistant uses OpenAI to provide personalized reef guidance based on your tank data and water parameters.

Features

🌊

Device Control

Real-time wavemaker control over local WiFi with live angle, speed, and motion profile management.

📊

Parameter Tracking

Automatic APEX probe readings and manual test logs unified on one dashboard with history charts.

🤖

AI Reef Assistant

Text and voice conversations powered by OpenAI, with full context of your tank data and parameters.

🔧

OTA Firmware Updates

Update device firmware over the air directly from the app.

Integrations

  • Neptune APEX — Live probe readings (pH, ORP, temperature, salinity, alkalinity, calcium, magnesium, nitrate, phosphate) with automatic daily snapshots
  • ReefIQ FlowMaster — Stepper-driven wavemaker with encoder feedback and multi-zone profiles
  • ReefIQ Mini — Compact reef monitor with TFT display

Data & Permissions

What data does ReefIQ access?

  • Account: Email, display name (via Firebase Authentication with Google Sign-In)
  • Tank data: Tank profiles, water parameters, and device configurations stored in Firebase
  • AI conversations: Text and voice messages processed by OpenAI for reef guidance
  • Microphone: Used for voice conversations with the AI assistant (audio is sent to OpenAI)
  • Local network: Used to discover and communicate with ReefIQ devices and APEX controllers
  • Bluetooth: Used for initial device setup and WiFi provisioning
